WildTrackAI is a collaboration between WildTrack and the UC Berkeley School of Information.

WildTrackAI aims to enhance the state of the art in non-invasive wildlife monitoring using artificial intelligence and computer vision.

Traditional wildlife monitoring relies on fitted instruments, marking, or capture - often with counterproductive effects on conservation.

WildTrack was founded on the premise that wildlife monitoring could be safer and more efficient utilizing non-invasive techniques inspired by indigenous people.

Learn More

The use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in wildlife monitoring to date has primarily focused on species recognition from whole body images. However, endangered species are often hard to find.

This initiative leverages AI in combination with WildTrack's Footprint Identification Technique (FIT - implemented in JMP software) to revolutionize endangered species monitoring using the evidence they leave behind ... Footprints!

How you can help

There are a number of ways to contribute:

Upload Images:

A large variety of good labeled images is essential to improve the performance of the model on current tasks as well as take on new tasks (for example: adding a new species, distinguishing between LF, RF, LH and RF foot). We typically look for a threshold of 100 images of a specific label type to include in the system.

The WildTrack website has helpful information on how to send images with more details on how to take high quality footprint images.

Note that we recognize that not all trails and footprints you come across are capturable as high quality images. We still urge you to send us what you are able to get. Even partial or lower quality images are useful to this project.
