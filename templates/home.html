TurtleTrack

TurtleTrack is a collaboration between WildTrack and the Piedmont Wildlife Center in North Carolina.

This collaboration was formed to help to monitor the population of the Eastern Box Turtle, a keystone speciec of Eastern North America.

WildTrackAI aims to enhance the state of the art in non-invasive wildlife monitoring using artificial intelligence and computer vision.

Traditional wildlife monitoring relies on fitted instruments, marking, or capture - often with counterproductive effects on conservation.

WildTrack was founded on the premise that wildlife monitoring could be safer and more efficient utilizing non-invasive techniques inspired by the San Bushmen of Africa.

Learn More

This initiative leverages AI in combination with WildTrack's Footprint Identification Technique (FIT - implemented in JMP software) to revolutionize Eastern Box Turtle monitoring using shells

How you can help

There are a number of ways to contribute:

Upload Images:

A large variety of good images is essential to improve the performance of the model on current tasks as well as take on new tasks (for example: adding a new individual or even a new species of turtle eventually) so any and all Eastern ox turtle images are welcome.

The Piedmont Wildlife Center's website has helpful information on how to send images with more details on how to take high quality images.

Note that while not all of your images will be high quality, even partial or lower quality images are useful to this project.
