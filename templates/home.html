Triangle Turtle Trekkers is a citizen science project created by Piedmont Wildlife Center with the goal of monitoring eastern box turtles across the NC Triangle region and beyond.

The International Union for the Conservation of Nature (IUCN) has classified eastern box turtles as a vulnerable species--meaning they are at risk of extinction in the wild. But you can help!

We need your help to find, photograph and monitor box turtles in and around your neighborhood, school, work, and other urban areas!

We are working alongside WildTrack to analyze these photos using FIT and AI softwares. We are testing these as non-invasive monitoring methods for box turtle populations.

How you can help

There are a number of ways to contribute:

Upload Images:

A large variety of good images is essential to improve the performance of the model on current tasks as well as take on new tasks (for example: adding a new individual or even a new species of turtle eventually) so any and all Eastern box turtle images are welcome.

To send images, please select the "report a sighting" tab in the menu or the observations page and complete the form.
