TurtleTrack is an initiative to monitor the population of Eastern Box Turtles, an ecologically important species, inhabiting the east coast of North America.

Unfortunately due to the progressive destruction of their habitat,

the population of the Eastern Box Turtle is declining drastically .

TurtleTrack is a collaboration between WildTrack and the Piedmont Wildlife Center in North Carolina.

It uses AI and Computer Vision to individually identify turtles based on pictures of their shells.

How you can help

There are a number of ways to contribute:

Upload Images:

A large variety of good images is essential to improve the performance of the model on current tasks as well as take on new tasks (for example: adding a new individual or even a new species of turtle eventually) so any and all Eastern box turtle images are welcome.

The Piedmont Wildlife Center's website has helpful information on how to send images with more details on how to take high quality images.

Note that while not all of your images will be high quality, even partial or lower quality images are useful to this project.
